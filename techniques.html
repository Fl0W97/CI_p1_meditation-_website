Consider these instructions as a brief introduction to the profound world of mindfulness. While diving deeper into resources like 'Search Inside Yourself' or other meditation guides can provide a richer understanding, let's kickstart your practice with some fundamental concepts. The goal is to initiate your journey, establish a mindful routine, and simply be present in the moment. Let's dive in and begin this transformative adventure together!"

Relaxation and alertness

The goal is to cultivate a balance between a focused and stable state of mind that remains soothing. Achieving this effortlessly requires practice, which is precisely what we're here to do.


Drawing from Tan's analogy in his book, envision your mind as a pot of water filled with sediment, constantly agitated. The result? Cloudy waters. But by ceasing the agitation and allowing the pot to settle, the water becomes calm and clear.

Practice, practice, practice, ...

Think of meditation as akin to hitting the gym for your mind. Just as lifting weights strengthens your body, meditation trains your mind to enhance its mental faculties. Through consistent practice, your mind becomes calmer, more perceptive, and capable of sustained focus, among other benefits.

Walking meditation, p.55

Start by standing still. Bring attention to this body. become aware of the pressure on the feet as they touch the ground. Take a moment to experience this body standing on the ground.
now, take a step forward. Lift one foot mindfully, move it forward mindfully, plant it down in front of you, mindfully, and shift your weight to this foot mindfully. take a short pause, and do it with the other foot.
If you like, when lifting your foot, you may repeat silently to yourself "lifting, lifting, liffting" and when moving and planting your foot forward, you may repeat silently to yourself, "moving, moving, moving".
After taking a number of steps, you may wish to stop and turn around. When your body in a standing position. If you like, you may repeat silently to yourself, "standing, standing, standing". As you turn around, do it mindfully, and if you like, you may repeat silently to yourself, "turning, turning, turning".
If you wish, you may synchronize your movement with your breathing. When lifting your foot, breathe in, and ehen moving and planting your foot breathe out. Doing this may help injext calmness into the experience.
